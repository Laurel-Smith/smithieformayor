As Mayor

I promise a "total reform" of the NYPD! I will work for New Yorkers to bring the NYPD into the 21st century. Time to stop a practise that treats the five boroughs like the Jewish gettho of the Venetian republic.

As we all know, stop and frisk or "Terry stop" is unconstitutional! And since more than 40% city residents are immigrants, it is my duty as Mayor to offer equal protection to citizens and non-citizens under the law. I will therefore issue special cards with the 4th, the 5th and the 14th amendment printed on it for all New York city residents' ID's.

Blue collar crime is more damaging to the city than petty crimes. I will leave no stone unturned tackling these high minded crimes, cyber crimes and other highly organized crimes.

I will put a cap on rent hike and property tax. Why? It is one of many ways to create a sustainable economy, jobs and affordable housing.

I will tackle obesity, solid waste management, carbon footprint and set up a Children's cabinet as way to boost prekindergarten education in all the five boroughs.

NYPD Reform

The organizational structure of the NYPD must be brought up to par with standards of the 21st century. So is operational standards and manners of policing our streets. Nepotism should be actively weeded out of the force. It is a bad recruitment practice that not only affect admin and professionalism, it is against the constitution. It is against the oath of allegiance and the city's charter from 1634 to 1855 till present.

For now, "a job for life" affects broad accountability in the force. Like an old engine that needs constant maintanance, NYPD budgetary allocations is mostly spent on maintaning old elephants behind the desk. As Mayor, I will reverse this habit. I'd turn the NYPD officer corps into a young mans' game. Spend more on better training, education and equipment, than keep old Elephants behind the desk.

I will establish a tranformational standard so young NYPD officers could carry out their duties with courtesy and professionalism and without being pressured to go overboard by "Elephants in the room". The NYPD is better controlled by the Mayor rather than a regional district attorney that is only answerable to the President.
